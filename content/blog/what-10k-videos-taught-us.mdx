## The view distribution most creators don't see

Half of all videos in our dataset hit fewer than 13,000 plays. The top 10% break 305,000. The top 1% break 3.4 million.

<StatGrid>
  <StatCard stat="13K" label="Median post" caption="Half of all analyzed videos sit below this." />
  <StatCard stat="305K" label="Top 10%" caption="The dividing line for 'this video worked.'" />
  <StatCard stat="3.4M" label="Top 1%" caption="The viral tier. 122 videos in our set cleared this." />
  <StatCard stat="69.8M" label="The ceiling" caption="The single highest-performing video in the dataset." />
</StatGrid>

The shape is heavy-tailed. About 18% of videos do not break 1,000 plays. About 28% sit in the 1K to 10K range. Then 32% land between 10K and 100K, which most creators would call "decent." Only 21% break 100K, and only 3.2% break 1M.

<Callout type="note" title="What this means in practice">
  If your last video did 8,000 views, you're closer to the median than to a flop. The reason it feels like a flop is that the algorithm shows you outliers all day. Outliers are not the baseline.
</Callout>

## The 1M-views bar is harder than people think

3.2% of the dataset cleared 1 million plays. 1.2% cleared 3 million. 0.27% cleared 10 million.

So when an "AI tool" tells you this video will probably hit 5M views, the prior is brutal. Without specific evidence pointing to a top-1% outcome, the realistic answer is "it'll do about 13,000."
