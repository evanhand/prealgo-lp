## The four steps

<Steps>
  <Step n="01" title="Gemini watches the video">
    Your file streams to Google's Gemini. It watches the full video and rates the components on a 1-5 scale: hook strength, value clarity, x-factor, format quality, execution. These ratings become a quality composite, a single number out of 100.
  </Step>
  <Step n="02" title="We pull real comparable videos">
    The system searches the database for videos that are about the same thing as yours, using embedding similarity (so it finds videos with similar substance, not just similar tags). Three lenses: most similar, highest views, most recent.
  </Step>
  <Step n="03" title="Anchor on your account history">
    If your account is linked, the algorithm pulls your own posting history. Your median post, your top 10%, your historical max. A creator whose past videos hit 1.7M shouldn't get predicted at the niche median of 30K.
  </Step>
  <Step n="04" title="Output 3 predictions per platform">
    A 3x3 grid: typical creator, your account, best case if it pops. Across all three platforms.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What it doesn't do

<Callout type="warning" title="Won't predict the long tail">
  View distributions are heavy-tailed. The very top end is genuinely hard to forecast in advance.
</Callout>

What it *will* tell you reliably: whether the video's quality and creator-fit are good enough that it could break out, given the topical and platform context. That's the question worth answering before you post.
