## 1. The hook makes no promise

The first 1-3 seconds need a specific claim or a specific curiosity gap. Generic intros don't trigger the algorithmic signal that the video is worth pushing.

<Compare
  badLabel="Weak"
  goodLabel="Strong"
  bad="Hey guys, today I'm going to talk about content strategy."
  good="The way most creators write hooks is making the algorithm punish them. Here's the fix."
/>

**The test:** read the first sentence out loud. Can someone tell what they're about to watch? If no, viewers can't tell either, and the early scroll rate (the percent who leave in the first 3 seconds) tanks the video before the algorithm gives it a chance.

## 2. There's no x-factor

X-factor is the share-trigger. The thing that makes someone send this video to a friend. Without it, even a polished video plateaus at the niche median.

<Callout type="note" title="X-factor is rarely production quality">
  Polished talking-head with no x-factor caps at B-grade reach. We see this constantly in the data.
</Callout>

The four x-factor types that consistently work:

## 3. Retention drops in the wrong place

Some retention drop is normal. Healthy short-form curves drop ~30-50% in the first 3 seconds, then settle. Unhealthy curves drop steeply *after* the first 3 seconds, which means the video set up an expectation it didn't pay off.

<RetentionCurve
  pts={[100, 78, 70, 65, 62, 38, 30, 26, 22, 20]}
  dropoffPct={50}
  dropoffLabel="major dropoff · promise broke"
/>

When the curve drops here, the video sold something at second 1 and stopped delivering at second 5. Recut from that timestamp, or reorder so the payoff lands earlier.

## 4. The format doesn't match the platform

A video built for TikTok pacing posted to YouTube Shorts often underperforms not because the content is bad, but because the rhythm is wrong.

## 5. Posting milestone content as if it were performance content

<Compare
  badLabel="Milestone (don't post to feed)"
  goodLabel="Performance (post to feed)"
  bad='"I finally hit 10K!" "Thanks for 500 followers!" "Day 87 of posting!"'
  good='Content with a take, a teach, a transformation, or a story. Something a stranger would watch.'
/>

Algorithmically, milestone posts read as low-information, low-share. Your existing audience may engage; new viewers will scroll. Five of these per month dilutes reach across the rest of your videos.

<Callout type="warning" title="Where milestones belong">
  Bio update. Stories. Pinned comment on your top post. Not the feed.
</Callout>
